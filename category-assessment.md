## ⚠️ Macro update that reframes everything: the Feb 2026 US–India tariff regime

A bilateral US–India trade deal (announced **Feb 2, 2026**) reset the tariff picture. Reported figures:

| Origin | Approx. tariff | vs. India |
|---|---|---|
| **India (electronics)** | **~18% cap** | — |
| India (general goods) | ~26% | — |
| **China** | **~54%** | +28–36 pts worse |
| **Taiwan** | ~32% | +6–14 pts worse |

**Two consequences — read both:**

1. **The thesis got stronger, not weaker.** The whole "manufacture in India instead of China" pitch is now a **~36-point** landed-cost swing (18% vs. 54%), not a marginal MFN difference. The China→India switching argument — central to every category below — is more compelling than when this project started.

2. **It supersedes our earlier duty numbers — correct them.** Earlier docs ([business-plan.md](business-plan.md)) leaned on "India not subject to Section 301" and "~0–5% MFN" duty. That framing is now outdated: India electronics face roughly **18%**, not low-single-digits. So the landed-cost math everywhere (including the **Appendix A worked example**, which used ~3.9% duty) **understates duty and needs updating** — on $13,000 of goods, ~18% is ~$2,340, not ~$507. The *relative* advantage holds; the *absolute* cost is higher. **Action:** re-verify exact rates per HTS and refresh the landed-cost examples.

## Validation findings (directional, from web research)

### LED / Lighting — ✅ validated as top pick
- **The USA is India's #1 LED-lighting export destination (~20% of exports)** — buyer density confirmed strong.
- Deep supplier base: large players (Bajaj Electricals, Surya Roshni, Wipro, MIC Electronics) plus hundreds of SME manufacturers/exporters on IndiaMart/TradeIndia.
- Verdict: the data backs the matrix — strongest all-around, and the tariff swing makes the switch pitch easy.

### Cable & Harness Assemblies — ✅ validated, strong for recurring/sticky revenue
- Deep ecosystem: tier-1s (Motherson Sumi, Yazaki India, LEONI India, Spark Minda) plus **export-ready SMEs already serving North America/Europe** (Sparsh–Pune, Jayshree–Ahmedabad, HTL, Avalon) holding **IATF 16949 / AS9100D / ISO 13485** certs.
- **Proof the model works:** Cypress Industries (a U.S. company) already runs India wire-harness manufacturing for U.S. clients — i.e., a direct analog of your business *and* a competitor worth studying.
- Verdict: confirmed — recurring, B2B-sticky, and a strong fit for your systems mindset.

### PCB / PCBA / EMS — ✅ supply depth de-risked, high upside
- India's EMS market ~**$39B (2026) → ~$62B (2031)**; PCBA exports growing at ~**30% CAGR**; total electronics exports jumped **$23.5B → $38.6B** in ~two years, with heavy government (PLI) backing.
- This **partly de-risks** my earlier concern that India's PCB supply was too thin — it's maturing fast.
- Verdict: highest value-add and best fit for your background; still the steepest learning curve, so keep as the stretch/3rd category.

## Recommendation (unchanged by the data, reinforced by it)

- **Lead: LED / Lighting** — deepest U.S. buyer pool (you're literally the #1 export market), strong India supply, biggest deals, cleanest path to the [D→C wholesale](model-c-b2b-wholesale.md) endgame, and the tariff swing makes the pitch nearly self-selling.
- **Second: Cable & Harness Assemblies** — recurring/sticky B2B revenue, export-ready suppliers, and a working precedent (Cypress) proving U.S. clients buy this from India intermediaries.
- **Stretch (3rd, later): PCB / PCBA** — your highest long-term differentiator; supply is maturing fast. Add once you have a deal or two of confidence.
- **Drop: Wired Audio** — too commoditized/China-entrenched, low value-add, easily disintermediated.
