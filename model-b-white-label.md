# Model B Playbook: White Label / Light Branding (Amazon-first B2C)

> **What this is:** You source an existing, proven product from an Indian manufacturer, apply light branding (your logo/packaging, minimal customization), and sell it direct to U.S. consumers — primarily on Amazon FBA. Faster and cheaper than private label, but you **are** the Importer of Record, and in electronics the **compliance burden (FCC/UL/CPSC/DOE) is the same as private label** — relabeling doesn't reduce your legal obligations.

> All numbers illustrative. Verify duties/compliance per product. Shared customs/compliance/landed-cost detail (incl. **CPSC eFiling effective July 8, 2026**) is in [business-plan.md](business-plan.md).

---

## Honest framing before you commit

White label on Amazon is the most *crowded* of your three options and the one where you compete most directly with entrenched Chinese sellers. The way to win is **not** to list a generic product and hope — it's to:
1. Pick a **non-wireless, lower-complexity** product (avoid FCC ID work and high failure rates),
2. Find a **specific niche/angle** where India has a cost or quality edge and big sellers are weak,
3. Treat the launch as a **data-gathering investment**, expecting **realistic net margins of ~8–18%** (not the gross), and
4. **Generate your own demand** rather than relying only on Amazon's organic traffic.

If you're capital-light, consider running [Model D (sourcing agent)](model-d-sourcing-agent.md) first to generate cash and supplier relationships, then fund this.

---

## Best first products (low RF complexity, lower return-risk)

From [business-plan.md §4](business-plan.md): wired audio accessories, non-smart LED, STEM kits (decide your age-target re: CPC), passive cables/adapters. These minimize FCC work and consumer-return headaches — the two things that kill beginner Amazon electronics sellers.

---

## Economics (illustrative)

- **Gross margin:** 20–40%. **Realistic net: 8–18%** after FBA fees, referral fee (~15%), PPC (often 10–20% of revenue at launch), and returns.
- **The number that matters is net after PPC.** Model your unit economics at **18% PPC and an elevated return rate** before committing — if it still clears ~15% net, it's viable. See the worked example in [business-plan.md §8](business-plan.md).

## Setup

1. **Entity, EIN, bank, Amazon Professional Seller account, customs bond + broker** (you're IOR).
2. **Product + supplier** via the validation and sourcing process below + [business-plan.md §6](business-plan.md).
3. **Compliance** for your category — non-negotiable. Budget UL/ETL for anything mains-powered; confirm whether LED drivers need FCC Part 15.
4. **Brand assets:** a simple brand name (trademark search first), logo, and **packaging with your importer name/address, country of origin, and required marks**.
5. **Amazon Brand Registry** (needs a trademark — file early; it unlocks A+ Content and brand protection).

---

## How to generate business (the core)

On Amazon, "generating business" = winning the keyword/ranking game *plus* driving outside traffic. Don't rely on organic alone.

### Channel 1 — Win Amazon search (the primary engine)
- **Listing optimization:** keyword-rich title, benefit-led bullets, high-quality images (infographics + lifestyle), A+ Content once brand-registered. Most beginner listings lose here first.
- **Amazon PPC, run like an engineer (your edge):** start with auto + broad campaigns to harvest converting search terms, then graduate winners into tight exact-match campaigns. Track **ACoS/TACoS, conversion rate, and click-share** in a spreadsheet. Your IT/data discipline is a genuine advantage over seat-of-the-pants sellers.
- **Honest review generation:** use Amazon's **"Request a Review"** button and the **Amazon Vine** program (enroll units for reviews). **Never** buy or incentivize reviews — it's a fast path to suspension.

### Channel 2 — Pick a niche angle, not a commodity
Differentiate so you're not in a pure price war with 50 identical listings:
- A **bundle** (e.g., wired earphones + carrying case + extra tips) that doesn't exist yet.
- A **specific use-case** variant (studio-monitoring earphones for musicians; a STEM kit aligned to a specific curriculum/grade).
- A **quality/spec upgrade** plus the certifications spelled out — buyers pay for trust signals in electronics.
- Mine **1–3 star reviews of top competitors** for their #1 complaint, then fix exactly that and say so in your listing.

### Channel 3 — Drive external traffic to your listing
Outside traffic boosts rank and reduces dependence on PPC:
- **TikTok / Reels / YouTube Shorts:** short demo/use-case videos. Cheap, high-leverage for visual electronics.
- **Influencer/creator seeding:** send free units to micro-influencers in your niche (musicians for audio, teachers/homeschoolers for STEM) for honest content + affiliate links.
- **Amazon Attribution + landing page:** run Meta/Google ads to a simple page or directly to the listing; Attribution tracks which external traffic converts.
- **Niche communities:** be genuinely helpful (not spammy) in relevant subreddits/forums/Facebook groups; Amazon also rewards off-site traffic with rank.

### Channel 4 — Expand the catalog (compounding)
Once one SKU works, **variations and adjacent SKUs** are the cheapest growth: more colors/sizes/bundles, then the next product your same buyer needs. A small family of related listings cross-promotes and lifts the whole brand.

### Channel 5 — Multi-channel once proven
After a winner is established on Amazon: **Walmart Marketplace, eBay**, and your own **Shopify** store (for brand + email list + repeat purchase). **Don't** open these before Amazon proves the product — Amazon is your cheapest demand-validation lab. See [business-plan.md §9](business-plan.md).

### Channel 6 — Build a retention asset
Capture buyers into an email/SMS list via **package inserts** (offer a warranty registration or a bonus guide — never a review incentive). Repeat buyers and a list you own are what turn a white-label SKU into a real brand over time.

## Validation-to-launch workflow

```
1. RESEARCH   → Helium 10/Jungle Scout: find a product with real demand, beatable competition,
                 and margin that survives 18% PPC. (business-plan.md §7)
2. VALIDATE   → Ethically: small real-inventory test on FBM/eBay, or a waitlist landing page.
                 NEVER take money for stock you don't have. (business-plan.md §7)
3. SOURCE     → Qualify Indian suppliers (IEC/GSTIN/certs/samples). (business-plan.md §6)
4. COMPLY     → Lock UL/ETL/FCC/CPSC as applicable; prep GCC/CPC data for ACE eFiling.
5. ORDER      → Small first run (100–300 units). 30/70 terms. Pre-shipment inspection.
6. IMPORT     → Broker files entry (you're IOR); goods to FBA.
7. LAUNCH     → Optimized listing + auto PPC; honest reviews via Request-a-Review/Vine.
8. DRIVE      → External traffic (TikTok/creators/Attribution); optimize PPC on real data.
9. DECIDE     → Winner? (steady daily sales, net margin holding) → reorder + expand catalog.
```

---

## First-90-days plan

- **Month 1:** Entity/Amazon setup; research + ethically validate 1–2 products; shortlist suppliers; start trademark for Brand Registry.
- **Month 2:** Samples + compliance checks; place first 100–300 unit order; build optimized listing + brand assets; line up creator seeding list.
- **Month 3:** Import → FBA → launch; run auto PPC + external traffic; gather reviews honestly; read the data and decide reorder vs. pivot.

---

## Metrics

- Conversion rate (target ~10%+ for electronics), sessions, click-share
- ACoS / TACoS and net margin **after** PPC and returns
- **Return rate** (keep it down — Amazon penalizes high rates and it wrecks margin)
- Organic vs. paid rank for target keywords; review velocity and rating
- Sell-through rate vs. reorder lead time (so you don't stock out or overstock)

---

## Risks and mitigations

| Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| **Crowded category / Chinese price competition** | Niche angle + differentiation + quality/trust signals; verify a real India duty edge per HTS before counting on it. |
| **Account suspension** | Strict compliance; honest reviews only; respond fast to performance notifications; don't gate behind a single account. |
| **Compliance is fully yours (IOR)** | Real UL/ETL/FCC/CPSC; certificate data ready for **CPSC eFiling (July 8, 2026)**; budget testing into landed cost. |
| **High return rate eats margin** | Choose durable, non-wireless products; sample every batch; pre-shipment QC; clear listings to reduce wrong-fit purchases. |
| **Cash flow (inventory + PPC vs. payout timing)** | Start small (100–300 units); don't over-order; keep a buffer; reinvest from proven winners. |
| **Thin real margin masked by gross** | Always model net after 18% PPC + returns before ordering; kill products that don't clear ~15% net. |
