# Reference: Operators & Educators to Learn From

> Real people and firms who run (or teach) the sourcing-agent / India-sourcing model. Study how they position, price, and operate. Honest caveat: truly U.S.-*based* operators in this exact India niche are relatively few — several of the best educators are based in Asia but serve U.S. clients. Location and focus are flagged so you can weigh relevance. The product line doesn't have to match yours; the *model* is what you're learning.
>
> Links were valid when collected (around the project start). Verify current roles on the live profiles. Part of Phase 0 of the [startup checklist](model-d-startup-checklist.md).

## How to mine these for value

1. **Listen to Meghla's podcast back-catalog first** — fastest, densest, free download of the India-specific playbook.
2. **Read AISG's and Panoramic's websites like a competitor** — note exactly what they emphasize (QC, on-ground presence, compliance) and what they charge for. That's your positioning research.
3. **Watch the China educators for operational mechanics** (vetting, negotiation, QC); use the India folks for market specifics.
4. **Connect on LinkedIn with a specific, genuine note** — reference something they actually said, not "pick your brain." Some share generously; a few may even refer overflow work.

> Takeaway: the prominence of China→India *tariff-arbitrage* messaging across these operators (Meghla especially) independently confirms the core thesis behind this whole plan — it's a real, actively-sold pitch.
